Annotated Bibliography - Anna Politkovskaya

Arkhangelsky, Andrey (2016)

Summary: Examines the lasting influence of Politkovskaya’s work and her assassination’s impact on Russian journalism.

Analysis: Provides insight into how authoritarian regimes suppress dissent through attacks on journalists.

Remnick, David (2009)

Summary: Covers the trial of Politkovskaya’s murder suspects and flaws in Russia’s judicial system.

Analysis: Investigative reporting that highlights corruption in political crime prosecutions.

Walker, Shaun (2016)

Summary: Explores how Politkovskaya’s assassination marked a turning point for Russian press freedom.

Analysis: Contextualizes her murder within the decline of independent journalism under Putin.

Amnesty International USA

Summary: Overview of Politkovskaya’s reporting on Chechnya and her murder.

Analysis: Highlights the threats journalists face and Amnesty’s advocacy for justice.

UNESCO Courier (2006)

Summary: Tribute to Politkovskaya’s courage in exposing human rights abuses.

Analysis: Highlights her global significance and the risks of investigative journalism.

Political Communication (2009)

Summary: Analyzes why Politkovskaya’s murder received little public outrage in Russia.

Analysis: Shows state control over public discourse and activism challenges.

Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty (2011)

Summary: Retrospective on Politkovskaya’s influence and press freedom five years after her murder.

Analysis: Tracks how journalistic freedoms eroded after her death.

Carnegie Endowment (2006)

Summary: Discusses the political impact of Politkovskaya’s assassination.

Analysis: Connects her murder to repression patterns against journalists in Russia.

The Guardian (2016)

Summary: Reflects on Politkovskaya’s legacy a decade after her death.

Analysis: Highlights her moral courage and growing dangers for journalists.

Thompson, S. M. (2017)

Summary: Dissertation on Politkovskaya’s contributions to human rights journalism in Chechnya.

Analysis: A scholarly examination of her impact, making it a valuable academic source.
