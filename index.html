Clippings File: Sources & Annotations

Arkhangelsky, Andrey. "Murder in Moscow: Anna's Legacy."

Journal: Index on Censorship, vol. 45, no. 3, 2016, pp. 34-36.

Link: Murder in Moscow: Anna's Legacy

Summary: This article reflects on the impact of Anna Politkovskaya's assassination ten years later, emphasizing the ongoing challenges faced by journalists in Russia. Arkhangelsky discusses the moral choices confronting journalists and the importance of alternative opinions in a propaganda-dominated environment.

Analysis: This piece provides scholarly insight into the enduring significance of Politkovskaya's work and the climate of press freedom in Russia, offering a theoretical framework for understanding the risks associated with investigative journalism in authoritarian regimes.

Remnick, David. "The Accused."

Journal: The New Yorker, 23 March 2009.

Link: The Accused

Summary: This article delves into the trial of those accused of murdering Anna Politkovskaya, providing a detailed narrative of the courtroom proceedings and the broader political implications. Remnick examines the challenges in achieving justice and the message sent to journalists.

Analysis: This in-depth reporting offers a comprehensive look at the legal aftermath of Politkovskaya's assassination, highlighting systemic issues within the Russian judicial system and the perilous environment for investigative journalists.

Walker, Shaun. "The Murder That Killed Free Media in Russia."

Journal: The Guardian, 5 October 2016.

Link: The Murder That Killed Free Media in Russia

Summary: A decade after Politkovskaya's death, this article explores how her assassination marked a turning point for press freedom in Russia. Walker discusses the tightening grip of the Kremlin on media outlets and the chilling effect on journalistic independence.

Analysis: This piece provides context on the decline of media freedom in Russia post-Politkovskaya, illustrating the broader impact of her murder on investigative journalism and the challenges faced by reporters in authoritarian settings.

Simonov, Alexei. "The Struggle for Press Freedom in Russia: Reflections of a Journalist."

Journal: *Index on Censorship*, vol. 45, no. 3, 2016, pp. 69-74.

Link: The Struggle for Press Freedom in Russia

Summary: Alexei Simonov reflects on the state of press freedom in Russia, discussing the challenges faced by journalists in a media environment increasingly dominated by the Kremlin. He emphasizes the importance of independent journalism for exposing human rights violations.

Analysis: Simonov’s insights on the continuing struggles for press freedom in Russia, and his reflection on Politkovskaya’s legacy, add an essential layer to understanding the risks of investigative journalism in authoritarian regimes.
